## Things to flag about the proposal

1. **Personal-library is the majority tier on both sides** — roughly 17 of 41 images, 22 of 40 texts. That matches your taste file and the revised spec (no PL ceiling).
2. **Romanian share of texts**: 11 of 40 = **27.5%** — above the 25% floor. Dinescu + Brumaru carry it; if you want more, a third Romanian list (Arghezi / Blaga / Cărtărescu short prose) could come next round.
3. **Nocturne candidates (5)**: hiroshige-moon-pine-ueno, brassai-paris-nuit-steps, hopper-nighthawks, basho-withered-branch, cavafy-candles, ryokan-thief-left-moon. Below the 30-item floor but useful seed for a later dedicated `nocturne-canon` list.
4. **"Weird" bucket is light at 5 texts + no images** — intentional at this stage; Kharms, Ponge, and the more surreal Klee (Twittering Machine) carry the 10%. Can expand with a dedicated oddities list if you want more texture.
5. **Tags are provisional** — schema/taxonomy files haven't been materialized yet (`add-corpus-schema` is still a change proposal, not archived). Once that lands with ratified `themes.yaml` / `mood.yaml` / `register.yaml`, any out-of-vocabulary tags here get mapped or flagged per the drift-handling flow.
6. **What's missing that you named but I didn't slot**: **manga**. I left it out of the first round because: (a) canonical manga panels as single-image canon is shaky — manga mostly reads as sequence, not individual panels; (b) tier posture for manga panels is murkier than for museum-represented photography. Worth a separate conversation on whether you want individual panels (e.g., Tezuka, Otomo, Taniguchi) or a single-panel-per-series approach.
7. **Also not slotted**: Steve Jobs / Apple (text or image?), Brahms (not an image/text corpus item), Seinfeld, old movies, Radiohead — these are preferences that might shape rendering/theming rather than corpus items. Open question whether any belong here at all.

## Composition audit

- **Flavor split**: 8 visual-day, 7 text-day → ~53/47 (target 60/40, within tolerance for a sample).
- **Aligned nocturnes**: 3 of 15 (20%). Low because the pool only has two vertical-format nocturne images (`brassai-paris-nuit-steps`, `hiroshige-moon-pine-ueno`). As the pool grows, this ratio lifts.
- **Anchor reuse**: 0 — every triplet has a distinct anchor. Sustainable reuse will happen as we scale (one Bashō haiku anchors several different days).
- **Gallery-item reuse**: `piranesi-carceri-vii-drawbridge` appears in #8 (summary) and #14 (gallery); `hopper-nighthawks` only #8; `kharms-blue-notebook-10` in #12 (gallery) and #15 (anchor); `ponge-the-crate` in #4 (summary) and #15 (gallery). Reuse works; items feel different in different compositions.
- **Taste-file coverage**: Japan (3), midcentury photography (4), modern painting (3), vintage cities (2), science (1), architecture (1) on images; haiku (5), Cavafy (1), modernist (4), Brumaru (2), Dinescu (1), absurdist (3) on texts. Roughly balanced.
- **"Weird" register**: #12, #15, parts of #5 and #13 lean deadpan/absurdist. ~3 of 15 = 20% — slightly high for a 10% weird target but fine for a first sample where we're stress-testing the register.

## What to validate in review

For each triplet, two questions:

1. **Does the pair stand alone?** Close one eye and pretend the anchor doesn't exist. Do the Summary item + Gallery item make a composition by themselves?
2. **Does the anchor add something?** Now reveal the anchor. Does it change how you see the pair — name something, open a third thought — without *explaining* them?

If both = yes, the triplet works. If the pair is dead without the anchor, the triplet fails the invariant (reject). If the anchor feels redundant, the triplet is weak (accept but flag — we might want a different anchor).
